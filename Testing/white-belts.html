Testing Tips

Testing for 10th Gold belt is a step that all students must take. Being the first martial arts test that many students have ever taken, the thought might be daunting. Rest assured, however, that if you put in the time and effort to learn all of the material and be proficient in each aspect, the test will be very easy.

Tip 1: PRACTICE!! If all of the actions are muscle memory, your testing anxiety won't mess up the moves.

Tip 2: Breathe. If you start feeling flustered or overwhelmed, take a deep breath and center yourself before continuing on.

Tip 3: Confidence. If you are confident in what you are doing and do it well, even if it is wrong it will look better than doing the correct thing hesitantly.

Tip 4: Take Correction Respectfully. If you are given correction during a test, don't get mad. Take the correction and implement it as best as possible.

Tip 5: Don't Add Anything Unnecessary. If you are asked to do something by an instructor on the board, do only what they say without asking questions or doing anything extra.

10th Gold Belt Testing Requirements

Students must know and be able to recite the first three Bible verses at the start of belt testing.

  1. 1. Giecho Hyung Il Bu (Basic Form 1)
  2. 2. Back and Front stances
  3. 3. Three basic blocks executed while standing and moving forward and backward:
    • High Block
    • Low Block
    • Knife-Hand Block
  4. 4. Four basic kicks
  5. 5. Back fist into a reverse punch from back stance to front stance
  6. 6. Combination strikes using both hands and feet
  7. 7. Three grabbing techniques
  8. 8. The first three One-Steps
